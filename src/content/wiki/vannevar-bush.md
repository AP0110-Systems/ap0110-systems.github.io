**Vannevar Bush** (1890–1974) was an American engineer who, as director of the U.S. Office of Scientific Research and Development, coordinated American science during the Second World War. He is remembered here for a single essay.

## Key work in the library

- [As We May Think](/docs/as-we-may-think/) (1945) — the essay introducing the **Memex**, a personal device for storing one's records and building *associative trails* between them.

## Significance

The Memex is the headwater of the entire lineage in the [overview](/wiki/): [Engelbart](/wiki/engelbart/)'s [Augmenting Human Intellect](/docs/augmenting-human-intellect/) re-examined it directly, and it precedes [Licklider](/wiki/licklider/)'s 1960 work by fifteen years. Its emphasis on private, actively curated knowledge with the links between documents as valuable as the documents themselves anticipates both hypertext and the [LLM-maintained wiki](/wiki/llm-maintained-wiki/).
