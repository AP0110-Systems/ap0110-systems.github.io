## Summary

In *Man-Computer Symbiosis*, [Licklider](/wiki/licklider/) proposed that the deepest gains in computing would come not from automating people away but from a tight partnership in which humans set goals and frame problems while machines handle the routine, formulable work. He estimated that engineers and researchers spent most of their "thinking" time on clerical and mechanical tasks — the kind a computer could absorb — freeing human attention for judgment and insight.

## Why it matters

This is the headwater of the lineage that runs through [Engelbart](/wiki/engelbart/)'s augmentation work to the Independent Internet. Licklider reframed the computer as a *communication and thinking instrument* rather than a calculating engine — the premise on which every later networked, user-controlled system rests.
