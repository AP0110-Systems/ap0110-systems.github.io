## Summary

`design.md` is the newest and least-settled member of the [agent-readable web standards](/wiki/agent-readable-web-standards/) family — an alpha convention from Google Labs for declaring a project's design system (tokens, type, color, layout rules) to design-aware coding agents in plain Markdown. Where [AGENTS.md](/docs/agents-md/) tells an agent how to *build*, `design.md` tells it how the result should *look*.

## How it fits

It extends the same operator-authored, plain-text, well-known-file pattern from engineering to design. Its governance is the least mature of the family — a single corporation, in alpha.

## Why it matters here

`design.md` shows the [agent-readable web standards](/wiki/agent-readable-web-standards/) pattern still expanding into new domains, each a new facet of operator *control* — the defining axis of the Independent Internet.
