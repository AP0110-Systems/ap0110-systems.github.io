**Jeremy Howard** is an Australian data scientist and educator, co-founder of the research lab Answer.AI and of fast.ai. In 2024 he proposed [llms.txt](/docs/llms-txt/), a convention for a curated, machine-readable manifest that tells a language model what to read at inference time.

## Significance

Howard's proposal extends the [agent-readable web standards](/wiki/agent-readable-web-standards/) lineage — [robots.txt](/docs/robots-txt/), [sitemap.xml](/docs/sitemap-xml/) — from *crawling* to *inference*, inheriting the Sitemaps pattern of publishing an operator-curated manifest. It is part of the protocol surface of the Independent Internet, and a current example of how operators reclaim control over how machines read their sites.
