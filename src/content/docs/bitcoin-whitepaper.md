## Summary

[Nakamoto](/wiki/nakamoto/)'s nine-page paper described a system for electronic payments sent directly between parties without a financial institution, using a peer-to-peer network and a proof-of-work chain to prevent double-spending. Its core move was replacing *trust in an intermediary* with *cryptographic proof* verifiable by anyone.

## Why it matters

Bitcoin demonstrated that a globally shared ledger could operate with no central owner — the strongest practical proof of [decentralization](/wiki/decentralization/) to date. It is the "Own" in the Read-Write-Own framing and a direct input to the Independent Internet's trustless and ownership principles. The full whitepaper (PDF) is freely available at the linked source.
