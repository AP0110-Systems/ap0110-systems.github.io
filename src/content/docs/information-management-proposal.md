## Summary

[Berners-Lee](/wiki/berners-lee/)'s memo to CERN proposed a distributed hypertext system to manage information about the lab's people, projects, and documents without imposing a central database or rigid hierarchy. His manager's note — "vague, but exciting" — sits atop what became the World Wide Web. The design favored decentralized linking over central control, so that anyone could add a node or a link.

## Why it matters

The Web's original architecture was decentralized by default: no permission needed to publish or link. The Independent Internet argues that this property eroded over the Web 2.0 era and must be deliberately rebuilt — see [decentralization](/wiki/decentralization/) and the [Semantic Web](/wiki/semantic-web/).

The full proposal is freely hosted at the linked source.
