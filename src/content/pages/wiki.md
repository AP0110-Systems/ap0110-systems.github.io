This wiki is a small, public knowledge graph. The source library holds one page per primary document; these synthesized pages connect them into a single story. Explore them visually on the [Web 4.0 page](/wiki/web4/).

## The thesis

The [Independent Internet](/wiki/web4/) (Web 4.0) is the Internet as it was intended to be — decentralized, resilient, self-hosted, and free of corporate surveillance. It is the latest turn in sixty years of ideas.

## The lineage

- **Origins** — [Vannevar Bush](/wiki/vannevar-bush/)'s [As We May Think](/docs/as-we-may-think/) (1945) imagined the Memex: a personal store of knowledge with associative trails between documents.
- **Partnership** — [Licklider](/wiki/licklider/)'s [Man-Computer Symbiosis](/docs/man-computer-symbiosis/) (1960) reframed the computer as a thinking partner, and his "Intergalactic Computer Network" [memo](/docs/licklider-memo/) (1963) imagined autonomous machines cooperating.
- **Augmentation** — [Engelbart](/wiki/engelbart/)'s [Augmenting Human Intellect](/docs/augmenting-human-intellect/) (1962) made amplifying ordinary people's capability a research program.
- **Hypertext & the Web** — [Ted Nelson](/wiki/ted-nelson/)'s [Computer Lib / Dream Machines](/docs/computer-lib/) (1974) and [Berners-Lee](/wiki/berners-lee/)'s [Information Management: A Proposal](/docs/information-management-proposal/) (1989) put a decentralized, linkable document graph in everyone's hands; the [Semantic Web](/wiki/semantic-web/) (2001) pushed toward machine-readable meaning.
- **Ownership** — [Nakamoto](/wiki/nakamoto/)'s [the Bitcoin whitepaper](/docs/bitcoin-whitepaper/) (2008) and [Ethereum's Web3](/docs/ethereum-web3/) proved a shared ledger needs no central owner.
- **The critique** — [Zuboff](/wiki/shoshana-zuboff/)'s [surveillance capitalism](/wiki/surveillance-capitalism/) (2019) names what went wrong.
- **The protocol surface** — the [agent-readable web standards](/wiki/agent-readable-web-standards/) ([robots.txt](/docs/robots-txt/) 1994 → [sitemap.xml](/docs/sitemap-xml/) 2005 → [llms.txt](/docs/llms-txt/) 2024 → [AGENTS.md](/docs/agents-md/) → [design.md](/docs/design-md/)) and [Interpretable Context Methodology](/wiki/interpretable-context-methodology/) (2026) are how operators reclaim *control* — the missing axis of Web 4.0.
