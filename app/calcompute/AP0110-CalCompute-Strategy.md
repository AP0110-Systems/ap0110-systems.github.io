# AP0110 Strategic Positioning for CalCompute Initiative

## Executive Summary

AP0110 is uniquely positioned to serve as a **critical infrastructure partner and technical contributor** to the CalCompute initiative established by California Senate Bill 53 (SB-53). Our Independent Internet (Web 4.0) technology, Local Intelligence Infrastructure (LII), and commitment to safe, ethical, equitable, and sustainable AI directly align with CalCompute's core mission and requirements.

---

## CalCompute Framework Development Overview

### Consortium Structure

The consortium consists of **14 members**:
- **4 representatives** of UC & research institutions (appointed by Secretary of Government Operations)
- **3 representatives** of labor organizations (appointed by Speaker of the Assembly)
- **3 representatives** of stakeholder groups (appointed by Senate Rules Committee)
- **4 technology and AI experts** (appointed by Secretary of Government Operations)

Members serve without compensation but are reimbursed for necessary expenses. The consortium dissolves upon submission of the framework report.

### Required Framework Report Elements

The framework report due **January 1, 2027** must include all of the following:

**A. Infrastructure Analysis:** Landscape analysis of California's current public, private, and nonprofit cloud computing platform infrastructure

**B. Financial Planning:** Analysis of cost to build and maintain CalCompute, plus recommendations for potential funding sources

**C. Governance & Operations:** Recommendations for governance structure and ongoing operation

**D. Usage Parameters:** Recommendations for parameters for use, including process for determining which users and projects will be supported

**E. Workforce Development:** Analysis of state's technology workforce and recommendations for equitable pathways, including role of CalCompute

**F. Partnerships:** Detailed description of proposed partnerships with nongovernmental entities; must demonstrate compliance with subdivisions (c) and (d)

**G. Public Sector Workforce:** Recommendations for prioritizing use of current public sector workforce

### Funding & Appropriations

If CalCompute is established within the University of California, the University of California may receive private donations for the purposes of implementing CalCompute. The bill makes provisions operative only upon an appropriation in a budget act or other measure for its purposes.

### Opportunity 6: University of California Placement Strategy

**Position:** AP0110 infrastructure as **distributed nodes across UC campuses**, maximizing equitable access

**Existing UC Relationships:**
AP0110 has established partnerships with multiple UC entities, providing a natural foundation for CalCompute collaboration:
- **UC Riverside**: Office of Technology Partnerships
- **SoCal OASIS**: Southern California innovation and startup support
- **UC LAUNCH Accelerator**: Startup acceleration program
- **UC Berkeley**: Direct campus partnerships
- **Berkeley SkyDeck Pad-13 Incubator**: Premier UC Berkeley startup incubator

Additionally, all AP0110 founders are UC graduates, providing deep institutional knowledge and alignment with UC's mission. The UC system has a long history of building foundational internet technologies—from ARPANET to modern cloud infrastructure—making CalCompute a natural continuation of this legacy.

**Strategic Advantages:**
- **Established relationships**: Existing partnerships across UC Riverside, Berkeley, and SoCal OASIS
- **Multi-campus distribution**: AP0110's decentralized architecture naturally supports UC's 10-campus system
- **Institutional knowledge**: UC graduate founders understand UC culture, processes, and priorities
- **Research integration**: Each campus can host edge computing resources for local research needs
- **Student access**: Provides equitable computational access to UC students and researchers
- **Community connectivity**: Extends connectivity to surrounding communities through mesh networking

**Implementation Model:**
1. **Primary CalCompute hub**: UC Berkeley or UC San Diego (major research centers)
2. **Distributed edge nodes**: All 10 UC campuses as edge computing sites
3. **Community mesh extensions**: Connect underserved communities near UC campuses
4. **Research partnerships**: Leverage existing UC partnerships for AI research initiatives
5. **Accelerator/investor connections**: Utilize UC LAUNCH and SkyDeck networks for implementation support

**Benefits:**
- Leverages existing UC partnerships and infrastructure
- Builds on established relationships rather than starting from scratch
- Enables equitable access across California's diverse regions
- Supports UC's public mission while advancing CalCompute goals
- Demonstrates scalable model for national replication
- Honors UC's historical role in internet technology development

### Challenge: Consortium Preference for Traditional Cloud Providers
**Mitigation:**
- Emphasize unique value of decentralized, publicly-owned infrastructure
- Demonstrate cost and sustainability advantages
- Highlight compliance with SB-53 requirement for "fully owned" infrastructure

### Challenge: Technical Complexity of Decentralized Systems
**Mitigation:**
- Provide comprehensive technical documentation
- Offer phased implementation approach
- Demonstrate existing successful deployments

### Challenge: Funding Uncertainty (SB-53 requires appropriation)
**Mitigation:**
- Prepare multiple funding model scenarios
- Identify private donation pathways through UC
- Demonstrate cost-effectiveness to justify appropriation

### Challenge: Competition from Larger Technology Companies
**Mitigation:**
- Emphasize unique advantages: public ownership, sustainability, equity
- Highlight proven public benefit track record
- Leverage open-source community support
