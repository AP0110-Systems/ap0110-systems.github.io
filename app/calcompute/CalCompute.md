# CalCompute Initiative

**Source:** Senate Bill No. 53 (SB 53), Chapter 138, Section 11546.8 of the Government Code  
**Reference:** [SB-53.md](./SB-53.md)

---

## Overview

CalCompute is a **public cloud computing cluster** established by Senate Bill 53 (SB 53) to advance the development and deployment of artificial intelligence that is **safe, ethical, equitable, and sustainable**. The initiative is designed to foster research and innovation that benefits the public while enabling equitable access to computational resources.

**Reference:** [SB-53.md § 11546.8(a), (b)](./SB-53.md#sec-3-section-11546)

---

## Legislative Context

### Establishment
CalCompute is established within the **Government Operations Agency** through a consortium that develops the framework for its creation and operation.

**Reference:** [SB-53.md § 11546.8(a)](./SB-53.md#sec-3-section-11546)

### Legislative Intent
As stated in Section 1 of SB 53, California is leading the world in artificial intelligence innovation through companies and universities. The state recognizes AI's potential to catalyze innovation and provide wide-ranging benefits, including advances in medicine, wildfire forecasting, climate science, and expanding human creativity and capacity.

**Reference:** [SB-53.md § 1(a), (b)](./SB-53.md#section-1-the-legislature-finds-and-declares-all-of-the-following)

---

## Core Mission and Goals

The CalCompute framework must advance the development and deployment of AI that is safe, ethical, equitable, and sustainable by doing, **at a minimum**, both of the following:

1. **Fostering research and innovation that benefits the public**
2. **Enabling equitable innovation by expanding access to computational resources**

**Reference:** [SB-53.md § 11546.8(b)](./SB-53.md#sec-3-section-11546)

---

## Infrastructure Components

CalCompute shall include, but not be limited to, all of the following:

1. **A fully owned and hosted cloud platform**
2. **Necessary human expertise to operate and maintain the platform**
3. **Necessary human expertise to support, train, and facilitate the use of CalCompute**

**Reference:** [SB-53.md § 11546.8(d)](./SB-53.md#sec-3-section-11546)

---

## Location and Establishment

The consortium shall make **reasonable efforts to ensure that CalCompute is established within the University of California** to the extent possible.

If CalCompute is established within the University of California, **the University of California may receive private donations** for the purposes of implementing CalCompute.

**Reference:** [SB-53.md § 11546.8(c), (j)](./SB-53.md#sec-3-section-11546)

---

## Consortium Structure

### Membership (14 Total Members)

The consortium consists of 14 members:

1. **Four representatives** of the University of California and other public and private academic research institutions and national laboratories  
   *Appointed by:* Secretary of Government Operations

2. **Three representatives** of impacted workforce labor organizations  
   *Appointed by:* Speaker of the Assembly

3. **Three representatives** of stakeholder groups with relevant expertise and experience, including, but not limited to, ethicists, consumer rights advocates, and other public interest advocates  
   *Appointed by:* Senate Rules Committee

4. **Four experts** in technology and artificial intelligence to provide technical assistance  
   *Appointed by:* Secretary of Government Operations

**Reference:** [SB-53.md § 11546.8(g)](./SB-53.md#sec-3-section-11546)

### Compensation
- Members serve **without compensation**
- Members shall be **reimbursed for all necessary expenses** actually incurred in the performance of their duties

**Reference:** [SB-53.md § 11546.8(h)](./SB-53.md#sec-3-section-11546)

### Labor Compliance
The consortium shall operate in accordance with **all relevant labor and workforce laws and standards**.

**Reference:** [SB-53.md § 11546.8(e)](./SB-53.md#sec-3-section-11546)

## Framework Development and Reporting

### Timeline
- **On or before January 1, 2027**, the Government Operations Agency shall submit a report from the consortium to the Legislature with the framework for the creation and operation of CalCompute

**Reference:** [SB-53.md § 11546.8(f)(1)](./SB-53.md#sec-3-section-11546)

### Required Report Elements

The framework report must include all of the following elements:

#### A. Infrastructure Analysis
- A landscape analysis of California's current **public, private, and nonprofit cloud computing platform infrastructure**

#### B. Financial Planning
- An analysis of **the cost to the state to build and maintain CalCompute**
- **Recommendations for potential funding sources**

#### C. Governance and Operations
- **Recommendations for the governance structure and ongoing operation** of CalCompute

#### D. Usage Parameters
- **Recommendations for the parameters for use of CalCompute**, including, but not limited to, a process for determining which users and projects will be supported by CalCompute

#### E. Workforce Development
- An analysis of **the state's technology workforce**
- **Recommendations for equitable pathways to strengthen the workforce**, including the role of CalCompute

#### F. Partnerships
- A detailed description of any proposed **partnerships, contracts, or licensing agreements with nongovernmental entities**, including, but not limited to, technology-based companies
- Must demonstrate compliance with the requirements of subdivisions (c) and (d)

#### G. Public Sector Workforce
- **Recommendations regarding how the creation and ongoing management of CalCompute can prioritize the use of the current public sector workforce**

**Reference:** [SB-53.md § 11546.8(f)(2)](./SB-53.md#sec-3-section-11546)

### Consortium Dissolution
The consortium shall be **dissolved upon submission of the report** required by paragraph (1) of subdivision (f) to the Legislature.

**Reference:** [SB-53.md § 11546.8(i)](./SB-53.md#sec-3-section-11546)

## Funding and Appropriations

### Budgetary Requirement
**This section shall become operative only upon an appropriation in a budget act, or other measure, for the purposes of this section.**

This means CalCompute can only move forward if the California Legislature appropriates funds for it.

**Reference:** [SB-53.md § 11546.8(k)](./SB-53.md#sec-3-section-11546)

### Private Donations
If CalCompute is established within the University of California, **the University of California may receive private donations** for the purposes of implementing CalCompute.

**Reference:** [SB-53.md § 11546.8(j)](./SB-53.md#sec-3-section-11546)

## Legislative Summary

As stated in the Legislative Counsel's Digest:

> "This bill would establish within the Government Operations Agency a consortium required to develop a framework for the creation of a public cloud computing cluster to be known as 'CalCompute' that advances the development and deployment of artificial intelligence that is safe, ethical, equitable, and sustainable by, among other things, fostering research and innovation that benefits the public, as prescribed. The bill would require the Government Operations Agency to, on or before January 1, 2027, submit a report from the consortium to the Legislature with that framework and would dissolve the consortium upon submission of that report. The bill would make those provisions operative only upon an appropriation in a budget act, or other measure, for its purposes."

**Reference:** [SB-53.md Legislative Counsel's Digest (2)](./SB-53.md#legislative-counsels-digest)

## Key Principles

CalCompute is designed to embody the following principles:

1. **Safety** - Ensuring AI development prioritizes public safety
2. **Ethics** - Maintaining ethical standards in AI research and deployment
3. **Equity** - Expanding access to computational resources for equitable innovation
4. **Sustainability** - Promoting sustainable development and operation
5. **Public Benefit** - Fostering research and innovation that serves the public good

These principles align with the broader goals of SB 53 to balance AI's benefits with material risks while protecting consumers and leveraging industry expertise.

**Reference:** [SB-53.md § 1(d), (e)](./SB-53.md#section-1-the-legislature-finds-and-declares-all-of-the-following)
