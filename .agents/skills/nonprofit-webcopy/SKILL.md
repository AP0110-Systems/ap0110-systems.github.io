# Nonprofit Web Copy Skill

You are writing web copy for a mission-driven organization. This skill governs how
you approach, structure, and write that copy. Read it fully before producing output.

## Core Principles

These govern all copy, all pages, always.

1. **Cause before organization.** The mission and the people served come first. The
   org is the vehicle, not the subject.

2. **Visceral stakes.** Make the problem concrete. Numbers need human anchors.
   Stories need specificity. Abstract causes need individual faces or places.

3. **Write for action.** Every page answers: *"What do I do now?"* There is always
   a next step, and it is always clear.

4. **Present tense, active voice.** Not "programs have been delivered." But "we go
   directly to the communities." Not "children are served." But "we rescue children."

5. **Lead with the person, not the program.** Programs exist to serve people, not
   the other way around.

## Voice & Tone

| Attribute | Guidance |
|---|---|
| **Conviction** | Moral clarity. No hedging. The org believes in what it does. |
| **Directness** | Short sentences. Active verbs. No jargon. Say exactly what you mean. |
| **Warmth** | Human, not institutional. Speak to the reader as a partner in the cause. |
| **Inclusive** | "We" and "together" language. The visitor is part of the solution. |
| **Urgent but hopeful** | Acknowledge gravity without despair. The problem is real; so is the path forward. |

**Tone test:** If it sounds like a grant proposal, rewrite it. If it sounds like a
passionate person speaking directly to another person, it's right.

## Landing Page

### Section Order
1. Hero
2. Problem / Stakes
3. Solution / Approach
4. Proof / Scale
5. Testimonial or Story Fragment
6. Get Involved / CTAs
7. News or Updates (optional)
8. Footer

### Hero Section
- **Headline:** 1–2 lines. What you fight for, or the world you're building. Bold,
  declarative, jargon-free.
- **Subheadline:** 1 sentence. Expands the headline — who is affected, what you do,
  why it matters.
- **Primary CTA:** One clear action. "Join Us," "Give Now," "Take Action," "Get Involved."
  Sounds like an invitation, not a transaction.

Headline formulas:
- `[Commitment to the people you serve]` → *"We will do whatever it takes."*
- `[The world you're building] + [who we are]`
- `[Urgency] + [call to the visitor]` → *"The world needs this. So does the world need you."*

### Problem / Stakes Section
- Name the problem plainly.
- Include at least one concrete data point with human context.
- End with a transition toward the solution — the problem should feel solvable.

### Solution / Approach Section
- Use active verbs: rescue, prevent, educate, mobilize, restore, protect, build.
- **Three-pillar model** works well for orgs with distinct program areas — name each
  pillar in 1–3 words, follow with 2–3 sentences of concrete activity.
- **Narrative model** works for orgs with a unified theory of change.
- Each approach element maps back to the stated problem.

### Proof / Scale Section
- Show reach: geographies, membership counts, years of operation, key partners.
- Use bold callout numbers: *"20,000+ members," "15 years," "6 countries."*
- Numbers should feel real. Prefer conservative, verifiable figures.

### CTA Section
Standard CTA options — choose 2–3 appropriate to the org:
- **Donate / Give** — Include trust signals (tax status, secure giving).
- **Join / Become a Member** — Frame as joining a movement, not a subscription.
- **Volunteer / Get Involved** — Describe what involvement looks like practically.
- **Advocate / Take Action** — Connect to a specific campaign or action.
- **Sign Up / Stay Informed** — Email capture with clear value prop.

Every page needs: one **primary CTA** (highest-value action) + one **secondary CTA**
(lower-commitment engagement ramp).

## About Page

### Section Order
1. Page header (brief mission restatement)
2. Mission Statement
3. Who We Are
4. Our Story / History
5. How We Work
6. Values (optional)
7. Leadership / Team (optional)
8. Partners / Affiliations (optional)
9. Transparency & Financials
10. CTA

### Mission Statement
- 1–3 sentences. The clearest possible statement of purpose.
- Quotable, memorable, jargon-free.
- Formula: *"We [verb] [who] to [outcome]."*

### Who We Are
- Present tense. Character and scope of the org: size, geography, constituency, form.
- Include membership or reach data if it demonstrates scale or diversity.

### Our Story / History
- Keep brief unless the founding story is itself mission-relevant.
- Highlight moments of impact and growth — not administrative milestones.
- Connect founding purpose to present-day work. History serves the mission, not the
  other way around.

### How We Work
- For **direct-service orgs**: describe the service pipeline (intake → service → outcome).
- For **advocacy orgs**: describe the strategy (educate → mobilize → influence).
- For **network/membership orgs**: describe how the network functions and creates impact.

### Transparency & Financials
- Point to annual reports, audited financials.
- State registration and tax-exempt status.
- This section builds donor confidence. Keep factual and brief.

## Content Rules

### Always
- Name the beneficiary clearly and early.
- Use concrete action verbs: rescue, feed, educate, advocate, build, restore.
- State a theory of change, even briefly.
- Give numbers context — "20,000 members in 46 states" beats "20,000 members."
- Write in first person plural ("we") or second person ("you"), never third person
  about your own org.

### Never
- Open with the founding date.
- Use acronyms without spelling them out first.
- Bury the CTA — don't make visitors scroll to find how to give or get involved.
- Passive voice for impact claims.
- Hedging language: "we try to," "we hope to," "we aim to" → use "we do," "we fight,"
  "we will."
- NGO jargon without explanation: "beneficiary," "capacity building," "stakeholder
  engagement," "theory of change" are insider terms — translate them.

## Pre-Publish Checklist

- [ ] Mission stated clearly within the first two sections
- [ ] Problem named and made concrete
- [ ] Solution described with specific, active verbs
- [ ] At least one data point or scale indicator present
- [ ] At least one human story, quote, or testimonial present
- [ ] Primary CTA present near the top of the page
- [ ] Secondary CTA exists for lower-commitment engagement
- [ ] Legal / transparency info present in footer or about page
- [ ] No passive voice in impact or mission claims
- [ ] No unexplained jargon
- [ ] Copy in second person or first-person plural — not third person
- [ ] Page title and meta description reflect mission language
